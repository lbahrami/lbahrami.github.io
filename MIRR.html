Building Resilience Together:

Community-Military Collaboration on Resilience in Washington, D.C.





Project Overview

As a resilience expert, I led the assessment of vulnerabilities related to flood scenarios for the Military Installation Resilience Review (MIRR) in Washington, D.C. My role focused on evaluating the risks posed by riverine, pluvial, and marine flooding to critical infrastructure and communities, using advanced geospatial and climate modeling techniques.

As the leader responsible for the geospatial components and assessment of the Environmental Justice (EJ) study for PECO, I played a critical role in identifying spatial disparities and opportunities within disadvantaged communities in Southeastern Pennsylvania. My expertise in geospatial technologies and environmental assessment was pivotal in analyzing complex data sets, mapping EJ areas, and providing actionable insights to promote equitable energy access.

Project Objectives

Evaluate "outside-the-fence" vulnerabilities impacting operations. Address climate hazards like flooding, extreme heat, and ice storms. Develop equitable, actionable resilience measures.

Methodology

1. Geospatial Analysis: Mapped critical infrastructure risks under future climate scenarios. 2. Vulnerability Assessment: Evaluated dependencies, exposure, and community vulnerabilities. 3. Stakeholder Engagement: Collaborated with advisory committees and community stakeholders. 4. Resilience Planning: Prioritized 14 key measures, including stormwater retrofits, blue-green infrastructure, and transit connectivity.
