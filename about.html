About Me

I am a climate-focused urban researcher and designer specializing in microclimate modeling, Geo-AI, environmental simulation, geospatial analytics, and climate-adaptive design. My research integrates urban planning, environmental physics, and data science to examine how nature-based solutions and spatial design shape thermal comfort, active living, public health, and equity in urban environments.


My work advances hybrid physics–AI modeling frameworks that link heat mitigation, ecological and carbon footprint reduction, and health outcomes, with a particular focus on heat-vulnerable populations and climate-exposed neighborhoods. Through advanced methodologies in GIS, machine learning, remote sensing, and spatial statistics, I develop actionable, data-driven strategies to support the creation of equitable, resilient, and low-carbon urban systems under climate change.
